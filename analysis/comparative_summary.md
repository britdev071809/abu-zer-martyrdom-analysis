## Source Criticism
### Chain of Transmission (Isnād)
- **Al‑Rabadha**: Early, well‑attested chains through Medina‑based scholars.
- **Istanbul**: Later, often solitary (gharīb) chains with Ottoman‑era transmitters.

### Internal Consistency
- **Al‑Rabadha**: Fits the political and geographical context of mid‑7th‑century Arabia.
- **Istanbul**: Requires an otherwise unattested journey to Byzantine territory; may reflect symbolic or legendary motifs.

### External Corroboration
- **Al‑Rabadha**: Supported by multiple independent early sources (Ibn Saʿd, al‑Balādhurī, etc.).
- **Istanbul**: Limited to late Ottoman chronicles and local folklore; absent from classical Arabic historiography.

## Historical Context & Motivations
### Why Two Narratives?
- **Al‑Rabadha**: Represents the mainstream, scholarly consensus rooted in early Islamic historiography.
- **Istanbul**: May have arisen from:
  1. Ottoman attempts to claim a direct connection with the Prophet’s companions.
  2. Popular stories that relocated holy figures to sites of later Islamic conquests.
  3. Confusion with other individuals named Abu Dharr or with similar epithets.
